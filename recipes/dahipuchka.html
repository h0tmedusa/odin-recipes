Dahi Puchka

Dahi Puchka also known as Dahi Puri is a richer variant of the classic Indian delicasy called puckha (pani puri). Available exclusively in India, this amazing dish used to be a royal favourite in the old times. Ridiciously available all over the country today, dahi puchka stands unbeaten as the best Indian Street food there is! Made of material that is readily available, it is very cheap and common all over the country.

India is a land of many cultures and many spices. There are hundreds and even thousands of different types of street foods all over the country matching the local culture and style. Some of those are so popular and publically loved that they are considered a kind of "Indian National Street Food" and Dahi Puchka clearly tops the list uniting the country spreading the message that no matter how different our cultures might be, we are all united as Indian. This dish is special in so many ways, so without further ado, lets find out how you can make it at home!

Ingredients

Here is the list of things you need to be ready with:

  • Flour: Wheat flour and refined Flour
  • Lime: Three-four whole lime along with imli
  • Spices and seasoning: Salt (black and plane), pepper powder(black and red), garam masala and the "puchka paani masala pack"
  • Potatoes: Five to ten white potatoes
  • Onion and garlic: Two to three onions and two cloves of garlic
  • Toppings: Bhujiya and other Toppings
  • Dahi: Desi dahi, better to have homemade dahi
  • Chutney: Green chutney, Red chutney, Sweet Chutney, Sour chutney

How to make Dahi Puchka

Let's put two and two together:

  1. Make small puris with the flour mix and get those puchkas ready.(same as with simple pani puri)
  2. Boil the potatoes, peel them and mash them together.
  3. Add the spices to it.
  4. Add chopped onions and some of the puchka.
  5. If the mash is dry, add some of the puchka pani we are about to make.
  6. To two litres of water, add lime, masala, imli and keep it overnight.
  7. Add whatever you can find the water.(if you can find human sweat and some drain water add those too)
  8. Now take the puchka, add the potato mash into it.
  9. Add Dahi.
  10. Drizzle some of the topping.
  11. Add all the chutneys accorind to taste.
  12. Rain some of the chopped onions over it. (again, add sweat if you can)

There you have it! Your dish ready to be served.
